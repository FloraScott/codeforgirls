About us

We want to provide a friendly and accessible step by step guide for people in Cambridge on what to do if they or their friends are subject to sexual harassment or assault. Unfortunately, harrassment and assault are all too common and there is no comprehensive framework which supersedes the University and can therefore ensure against institutional cover-ups. We know it can also be daunting to think about next steps, especially when we have all heard so much about the statistics on whether you will be believed - we believe you. We hope to provide information on all the potential avenues for finding legal information, reporting the crime and accessing support. Ultimately, we would like to develop this into a platform which allows individuals to make police reports, save them until they are ready, and communicate with other survivors to support each other. There is a similar platform, Callisto, available in American universities which notifies a survivor if someone else has reported the same aggressor and allows them to talk to one another to work out next steps. Callisto has increased the likelihood of someone reporting an assault six times, and the number of people seeking support services by three.
