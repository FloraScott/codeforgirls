University Counselling Services (UCS)

SAHA: Sexual Assault and Harassment Advisor

The SAHA is a specialist support worker who provides emotional and practical support to anyone who has been raped, sexually assaulted, or harassed, recently or in the past. We can support you whether this happened at University or not, and can help you to access other available support services. The SAHA can support you to explore your options for reporting what has happened and can support you through this process if you do choose to go ahead. You do not have to report your experience to access support from the SAHA.

To arrange a SAHA appointment at UCS please complete a Pre-SAHA Form. You do not need to give long answers; you can also put "I would prefer to talk about this in person" or "Prefer not to say", if it is difficult to write about.

Email: sexual.assault.advisor@admin.cam.ac.uk

Other information

The UCS runs three kinds of groups:

  • Workshops
  • Focused groups
  • Larger counseling groups

These workshops are generally one-off events and do not require any ongoing comittment. A timetable for workshops occuring during Lent Term 2020 is available, and to register your interest in attending a group or workshop, please complete the Groups or Workshop Registration Form online.
